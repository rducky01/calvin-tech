Politwoops Case Overview

This case involves a handful of ethical issues relating to data ownership. Specifically, when a Twitter user deletes a tweet, does he have the right to have that data removed from elsewhere on the internet? This is an interesting question with several different ethical implications to be taken into account.

In late 2015, Twitter began blocking access to its application programming interface (referred to as an api) for a number of accounts belonging to Politwoops for violating Twitter’s terms of service. The organization was using the data downloaded via the api to archive deleted tweets of politicians. Overnight the Politwoops service stopped working, causing news feeds to go blank. Of course, Twitter’s action drew the ire of various watchdog groups on the internet, and this ultimately led to the reversal of Twitter’s decision.

In this case analysis we will analyze the situation from the perspective of three main characters; The perspective of the politician, who would like to have his or her tweet deleted, the perspective of the Twitter organization, who thought Politwoops was violating its policy, and the perspective of the Politwoops organization,, who feel they are doing the right thing by archiving the things these politicians delete and are potentially trying to backtrack on. In doing so, we’ll identify three ethical approaches that these characters are taking and explain each one in great detail.

The Politician's Perspective

From the politician’s perspective, he or she is concerned with ensuring that a certain tweet that was posted from the politician’s account is erased. He or she is concerned about the political or cultural ramifications of the tweet, and would like to avoid any fallout that might occur. The politician believes that since they are tweeting from their own account, that they have the right to redact what they say publicly, and have the tweet deleted. Thus the politician is taking a rights based approach to the issue.

This approach might be seen as controversial by some, who might argue that by deleting the tweet the politician is trying to hide their true intentions, edit the historical record, and manipulate the sentiments of the politician's constituency. This may not bode well for the politician’s public image.

The Twitter Organization's Perspective

From the Twitter organization’s perspective, all of its users have a right to the same level of privacy. Part of this privacy includes having the ability to delete tweets. In turning off access to the api, they were helping the user to that end. In doing this Twitter was taking a fairness and justice approach.

The Politwoops Organization's Perspective

From the Politwoops perspective, the public has a right to know what the politicians are tweeting about, and by archiving deleted tweets, they are keeping the public informed. This does a service to society and helps avoid cases where a politician may want to hide the truth. This can be seen as a utilitarian approach.

Rights Based Approach

With the rights based approach, one could argue that certain rights are inalienable, and set by society. In the case of the politician posting on Twitter, one inalienable right that could come into play is the freedom of speech. As the owner of the tweet, the politician has the right to have all instances of the tweet deleted. Within the perspective of the politician, this should include copies of the tweet in theory.

This may be a tall order because everything that happens online is ultimately stored in a log on a server somewhere, and once a tweet is posted it’s fair game for download. Perhaps something is said that is outrageous and someone wants to save it so that they have proof that the comment was made? All they have to do is take a screen shot. So I’m not sure how feasible this may be. Twitter’s answer was to shut off the stream of deleted tweets through the api.

Fairness and Justice Approach

With the fairness and justice approach all individuals are treated equally and fairly. As such Twitter felt that it was acting justly in blocking access to deleted tweets from all users, not just the politicians. Twitter felt that as the owner of the tweets, the user should be able to have control over their content.

But was this fair to the Politwoops organization? I’m not so sure. By performing this action, I feel that Twitter came dangerously close to enacting censorship which is a dangerous precedent to set in today’s political climate.

Utilitarian Approach

With the utilitarian approach actions are done which provide the best benefit of society and the least amount of harm. In the case of the Politwoops organization, the organization was acting in the interest helping society by collecting the deleted tweets. The aim was to keep the politicians on a level playing field by keeping all tweeted information in the public arena. This was to act as a sort of check or balance and to aid in objective journalism.
