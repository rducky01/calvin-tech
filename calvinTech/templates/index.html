About Me

Skylar Calvin is a Senior Service Desk Analyst at NIC Inc. (NASDAQ: EGOV), a leading provider of government technology solutions. He is responsible for leading the Service Desk team in day to day operations and working support tickets at an escalated level. In addition to these responsibilities, he has also been able to use his background in Mathematics and Data Science to design and implement several custom Key Performance Indicators that have become crucial to the success of the Service Desk and the division to which it reports.

Skylar received a Master of Science degree in Data Science and Analytics from the Institute for Data Science and Informatics at the University of Missouri Columbia. His emphasis area is in High Performance Computing and he has completed course work in applied machine learning, cloud computing, and advanced data visualization. His capstone project used a Poisson distribution to effectively predict hot spots of crime around Kansas City, MO, using historical crime data from the KCPD between 2016 and 2019, socioeconomic data from the US Census Bureau’s American Community Survey, weather pattern data from the National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration, in addition to data from a number of other sources. Using the resulting model he was able to accurately project hot spots of certain crimes side by side with actual data, using data from 2019 for validation.

Over the past three years, Skylar has been applying data science increasingly more in his day to day operations. In his current role at NIC Inc, he created an ETL pipeline used for cleaning and storing Service Desk ticket data from multiple sources in a centralized database. He then used this to design and deploy a PowerBI dashboard for visualizing KPIs. This dashboard has been used by managers and directors alike organization wide. Additionally, using his machine learning prowess, he applied Natural Language Processing and used some unsupervised machine learning to find patterns of the type of work being done by certain departments, using only the manually entered description fields in the tickets. He then used the resulting clusters to build a classification model to sort the tickets in the catch-all “Other” ticket category into groups. This has led to increased awareness by departmental management and to more efficient ticket handling by support team members.

Overall, Skylar is a hard working, passionate data scientist with a keen attention to detail. His dominant character traits are confidence, persistence, and an intense level of curiosity.
